The Isabella
Stewart Gardner
Museum Heist


On March 18, 1990, 13 works of art were stolen from the Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum in Boston. Two men in police uniforms pushed the Museum buzzer, stated they were responding to a disturbance, and requested to be let in. The guard on duty broke protocol and allowed them through the employee entrance. At the fake officersâ€™ request he stepped away from the watch desk. He and a second security guard were handcuffed and tied up in the basement of the Museum. The haul is valued at $500 million and no arrests have been made. No works have been recovered. The museum is offering a $10 million reward for information leading to the art's recovery.
